Edition One Theme

Transformation

Essays that explore the moments that break us open and remake us whole.

  • Identity
  • Migration
  • Loss & Recovery
  • Reinvention
  • Family
  • Becoming

What we're looking for

"Transformation" is intentionally broad. Your essay might explore coming into yourself, leaving behind who you were, physical or metaphorical migration, what grief dismantles and what you rebuilt, career pivots, generational patterns, or the slow (or sudden) process of change.

The best essays find the universal in the specific. Show us your particular moment, and let it resonate outward.

We value

  • Honesty over polish — Raw truth beats literary performance
  • Specificity — Concrete details ground abstract ideas
  • Reflection — Not just what happened, but what it means
  • Risk — Essays that go somewhere uncomfortable or surprising

We're less interested in

  • Advice or prescriptive content ("here's how to transform your life")
  • Essays that resolve too neatly
  • Surface-level treatment of deep subjects
  • Writing that prioritizes cleverness over connection

Submission requirements

Essay specifications

  • Word count: 1,500–3,000 words
  • Format: Google Doc (preferred) or Word document
  • Title: Include a working title (we may suggest alternatives)

Author materials

  • Bio (50-100 words) — Written in third person
  • Headshot — High resolution (at least 800x800 pixels)
  • Links — Website, newsletter, social media

Editorial process

What to expect:

  • Acknowledgment — We'll confirm receipt within 48 hours
  • Review — We read every submission carefully (allow 2 weeks)
  • Decision — Accept, decline, or request revisions
  • Editing — Collaborative feedback on structure, clarity, pacing
  • Final approval — You sign off before publication

We're editors, not rewriters. We might suggest structural reorganization, cutting or expanding sections, or strengthening the opening or closing. You always have final say. We won't publish without your approval.
